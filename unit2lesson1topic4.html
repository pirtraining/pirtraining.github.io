Incident Timeline

Collate the facts you have gathered into the “incident timeline” table in the PIR report. See Table 2 for a partially completed example. Each data point in the timeline should be identified by a date and time. Choose a single time format and use it consistently throughout, particularly if it's possible for an event response to span multiple timezones or geographies. Do include links, graphs, charts, etc. if it helps to illustrate the event more clearly (see Table 1).

Date and Time Timeline Event
May 1 2024 12:00 GMT The server reported an ‘out of disk space’ error.
May 1 2024 12:01 GMT The server is restarted by the on-call engineer.
May 1 2024 12:05 GMT The server restart is complete. The on-call team monitor connection requests to the server.

Table 1: Excerpt from a possible incident timeline.

Piecing together the sequence of events before the PIR meeting is vital because it establishes a common understanding of the incident. Once we are all discussing the same issue, meaningful learning can then take place. Familiarising yourself with the timeline will also give you a sense of the flow of events, the scope of the incident, and the personalities involved. This information will prepare you to guide the PIR meeting and informs the types of questions to ask. Once your incident timeline is complete, you now have a draft PIR report and you are now ready to facilitate a PIR meeting!

Key Point: Document the facts you have discovered in an incident timeline, and use that information to guide your approach to facilitating the PIR meeting.
