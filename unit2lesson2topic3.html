After the PIR Meeting

As a PIR leader, here is a five-step guide to making the most of your PIR meeting:


1

Step 1) Iterate on the PIR report:

  1. Make any updates or corrections to the incident timeline in light of the PIR meeting.
  2. Summarise the main discussion topics covered during the PIR meeting. Ensure that this accurately reflects the areas covered during the meeting.
  3. Extract the learning points uncovered during the discourse. These are the core findings of your PIR report.
  4. Identify any action items agreed upon during the PIR meeting. Associate each action item with an owner.

Tip: A good rule of thumb for writing practical action items is to make them SMART—Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. However, the most essential part of any action item is the owner. If nobody is willing to take responsibility for the solution of a problem, then the implementation is doomed to be misunderstood, forgotten, or abandoned.

Step 2) You may optionally add other sections to your PIR report. For example, an executive summary can help to capture the reader’s attention and interest.

Example Executive Summary: On May 1st 2024, there were high processing delays across all regions due to a disk space error in the central data processing servers. As a result of the processing delays, there was a significant backlog of transactions and data processing tasks, leading to disruptions in the company’s operations.

The incident response team implemented a fix within 45 minutes, restoring full processing capabilities. To prevent similar incidents in the future, the engineering team is implementing a more robust monitoring system for the data processing infrastructure and conducting regular hardware maintenance and upgrades.

2

3

Step 3) Invite feedback on the revised PIR report and incorporate any necessary updates into the finalised PIR report.

Example Email: Hi everybody, a revised PIR report that includes the learnings and action items from yesterday’s PIR meeting is available [here]. Please check if anything is missing or misinterpreted. I plan to share the finalised PIR report tomorrow and arrange a schedule to follow up with action item owners. Thanks!

Step 4) Agree on a regular schedule for following up on action items—weekly, fortnightly, monthly, etc. Schedule a short recurring meeting with action owners to get status updates on open action items. Continue the sequence until all actions are complete.

Tip: Consider tracking action items as regular work items in your engineering workflow. Doing so will reduce the likelihood of deprioritisation or abandonment.

4

5

Step 5) Share the PIR report. How widely you can share the PIR report will depend on your organisation. Ideally, the PIR report can be externally shared so that we may collectively push software safety forward across the industry. At a minimum, the PIR report should be kept with past PIR reports and be searchable by staff.

Example Email: Hi all. As many of you know, we encountered a severe issue last week characterised by high processing delays across all regions. I’d like to thank the incident response team for their expertise in resolving the problem and their transparency in discussing the circumstances of the event.

We learned a lot from this situation, and a full write-up of the Post Incident Review is available [here]. This report will be added to our training documentation [here], and progress on follow-up items can be tracked [here]. Thanks!


PIR Leader’s Facilitation Essentials:

  • Iterate on the PIR report, filling in the details of the PIR meeting.
  • Make sure that action items have owners.
  • Schedule follow-up meetings with action item owners.
  • Share your PIR report as widely as possible.
