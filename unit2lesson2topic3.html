After the PIR Meeting

1

Step 1) Iterate on the PIR report:

  1. Make any updates or corrections to the incident timeline in light of the PIR meeting.
  2. Summarise the main discussion topics covered during the PIR meeting. Ensure that this accurately reflects the areas covered during the meeting.
  3. Extract the learning points uncovered during the discourse. These are the core findings of your PIR report.
  4. Identify any action items agreed during the PIR meeting. Associate each action item with an owner.

Tip: A good rule of thumb for writing effective action items is to make them SMART—Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. However, the most essential part of any action item is the owner. If nobody is willing to take responsibility for the solution of a problem, then the implementation is doomed to be misunderstood, forgotten, or abandoned.

Step 2) You may optionally decide to add other sections to your PIR report. For example, an executive summary can help to capture the reader's attention and interest.


2

3

Step 3) Invite feedback on the revised PIR report, and incorporate any necessary updates into the finalised PIR report.

Step 4) Agree a regular schedule for following up on action items—weekly, fortnightly, monthly, etc. Schedule a short recurring meeting with action owners to get status updates on open action items. Continue the sequence until all actions are complete.

Tip: Consider tracking action items as regular work items in your engineering workflow. Doing so will reduce the likelihood of deprioritisation or abandonment.

4

5

Step 5) Share the PIR report. How widely you can share the PIR report will depend on your organisation. Ideally, the PIR report can be shared externally so that we may collectively push software safety forward across the industry. At minimum, the PIR report should be kept with past PIR reports, and searchable by staff.


PIR Leader's Facilitation Essentials:
  • Iterate on the PIR report, filling in the details of the PIR meeting;
  • Make sure that action items have owners;
  • Schedule follow-up meetings with action item owners;
  • Share your PIR report as widely as possible.
