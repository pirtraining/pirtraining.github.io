Introduction

When software fails, we have been encouraged to search for a single root cause to explain what went wrong.

Now, thinking has changed.

With increasingly complex systems we must seek multiple contributing factors. And as we unravel these factors, rather than blame or point fingers, we have to take an approach that fosters learning and information sharing.

Throughout this course, we will refer to this approach as a Post Incident Review (PIR).

In this lesson, we will explore the complexity of software systems, how surprises occur, and how we can learn from them.



Lesson Objectives

After studying this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Define an IT incident.
  • Identify the purpose of a PIR.
  • Recognise the characteristics of team psychological safety.
