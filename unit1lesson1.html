Reviewing Incidents

“It takes many factors, all necessary and only jointly sufficient, to push a system over the edge of failure.”


—Dekker (2013, p.17)

Introduction

When software fails, we have been encouraged to search for a single root cause to explain what went wrong. Now, thinking has changed.

With increasingly complex systems we must seek multiple contributing factors. And as we unravel these factors, rather than blame or point fingers, we have to take an approach that fosters learning and information sharing. Throughout this lesson, we will refer to this approach as a Post Incident Review (PIR).


By sharing a deeper knowledge of the system, IT engineers will be empowered to make their jobs easier.
By establishing a commitment to continuous improvement, IT managers will build trust and resilience among teams.
By producing thorough records of incidents, IT organisations will discover patterns that will inform effective remediation measures and improve how future incidents are handled.



Lesson Objectives

After studying this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Define an IT incident.
  • Identify the purpose of a PIR.
  • Recognise the characteristics of team psychological safety.
