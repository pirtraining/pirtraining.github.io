Preparing for Post Incident Reviews

“When an unpredicted event occurs, we immediately adjust our view of the world to accommodate the surprise.”


—Kahneman (2011, p.202)

Introduction

In earlier lessons, we learned that the key role in a PIR is that of PIR leader. As PIR leader, you have two core responsibilities:

  1. To prepare a document that captures the details of the PIR—the PIR report;
  2. To facilitate a group discussion between those closest to the event—the PIR meeting.

This lesson is about preparation and will focus on the first responsibility, which is to lay the groundwork for an effective PIR meeting.



Lesson Objectives

After studying this lesson and the relevant recommended readings, students should be able to:

  • Identify the core components of a PIR report.
  • Distinguish opinions from facts when collecting data.
  • Recognise the importance of compiling an incident timeline in advance of a PIR meeting.
