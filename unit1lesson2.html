The Language of Post Incident Reviews

“Oops!... I did it again.”


—Spears (2000)

Introduction

In the previous lesson, we explored some examples of incidents, learned the purpose of a learning-based PIR, and highlighted the importance of team psychological safety.

The building blocks of creating an atmosphere of trust and respect is being aware of how we communicate with each other. In this lesson we're going to examine the vocabulary of PIRs.

We are going to discover how the language of description creates team psychological safety.



Lesson Objectives

After studying this lesson and the relevant recommended readings, students should be able to:

  • Recognise the consequences of assigning blame in a PIR.
  • Identify how asking people for descriptions contributes to learning.
