Glossary of Terms and Acronyms

  • A

    • Action Item: A specific, measurable task that must be completed as part of a larger project or goal.
    • Agent: A linguistic term to refer to the ‘doer’ of an action.

  • B

  • C

  • D

    • Deployment Issue: A disruption to normal IT services caused by releasing a software or system update.

  • E

  • F

    • Five Whys: An an iterative interrogative technique used to get to the true root cause of an incident by asking five ‘why’ questions.
    • Fundamental Attribution Error: The tendency for people to overemphasise the role of personal characteristics and underestimate the influence of situational factors when describing someone’s behavior or action.

  • G

  • H

    • Hardware Failure: The malfunction or breakdown of a computer’s physical components.
    • Hazard: A potential source of risk that could lead to an incident occurring.

  • I

    • Incident: An IT incident refers to an unexpected event that disrupts a software system, and points to an area of complexity in that system.
    • Incident Timeline: A chronological history of an incident.

  • J

  • K

  • L

  • M

  • N

  • O

  • P

    • PIR: Post Incident Review. A PIR is a template for learning from an incident in an environment of team psychological safety.
    • PIR Leader: Person responsible for writing up the details of the incident and facilitating a group discussion between those closest to the incident.

  • Q

  • R

    • RCA: Root Cause Analysis. A structured approach to incident investigation that aims to identify the fundamental cause of an issue or problem.

  • S

    • Security Threat: An event that jeopardises the confidentiality or integrity of an information system or the information it processes.
    • Service Outage: An unplanned interruption or reduction in the quality of an IT service.
    • Software Bug: An error, flaw, or unintended behaviour in a software application that causes it to produce incorrect or unexpected results.

  • T

    • Team Psychological Safety: A shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk taking.

  • V

  • W

  • X

  • Y

  • Z
