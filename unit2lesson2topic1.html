Before the PIR Meeting

1

Step 1) Identify a list of participants to attend the PIR meeting. Participants will be those directly involved in the incident timeline. These will be the people who:

  • Identified the issue (perhaps customer support or environments teams);
  • Responded to the incident (perhaps on-call engineers and infrastructure teams);
  • Managed the incident, or were responsible for verifying the resolution (perhaps incident commanders and quality engineers);
  • Understood the technical aspects of the incident (perhaps software developers and engineers);
  • Lead the service teams involved in the incident (perhaps product managers and engineering managers).

Tip: Try to avoid opening up the invitees to anyone who is curious or interested. We want to be inclusive, but we also need to have active participants. A full room of silent onlookers or blank screens on camera is likely going to be to the detriment of open discussion.

Step 2) Schedule a PIR meeting invitation for the list of participants. The PIR meeting should take place approximately three-to-seven days after resolution of the incident.

Tip: If you are not sure how long the meeting should be, then aim for a one-hour timeslot. In constructing the incident timeline, you will have a good sense of the depth of an incident. Use this information to guide how much time to allot.

2

3

Step 3) Prepare an agenda for the PIR meeting. The agenda will have two main sections:

  1. To begin, you will walk the group through the incident timeline;
  2. You will then lead a group discussion and ask participants to converge on shared learnings and actions.

Tip: It's helpful to scan through the incident timeline with this agenda in mind. Make a note of any areas that might benefit from clarification. Draft some open-ended questions that invite descriptions of interesting topics.

Step 4) Scout the meeting logistics.

  • Make sure any physical room bookings have sufficent capacity;
  • Check remote conferencing capabilities;
  • Consider running a test with a colleague to prepare yourself and the venue.

Tip: Run a test with a colleague to prepare yourself and the venue.

4

5

Step 5) Share the draft PIR report, including the incident timeline, to all participants before the PIR meeting takes place.

Tip: Inviting corrections and feedback at this stage will set a tone of openness and inclusion before the meeting even begins.


PIR Leader's Facilitation Essentials:
  • Invite participants to a PIR meeting within one week of the incident.
  • Familiarise yourself with the incident timeline and prepare an agenda for the PIR meeting.
  • Share the incident timeline with participants in advance of the meeting.
