Factual Data

Creating an incident timeline is a data collection activity. An incident timeline is a chronologically ordered sequence of key facts.

It’s important to stick to the facts because human recollection is fundamentally unreliable. In the words of Kahneman (2011, p.204), “the worse the consequence, the greater the hindsight bias.” The perspectives of those involved are certainly important, and will be heard during the PIR meeting, but the timeline should only consist of factual data.

Limiting ourselves to the facts also helps us to avoid the narrative traps outlined in the previous lesson about language.

Figure 3.1 Opinion or Fact:

Timeline event:

“Someone probably forgot to clean out old files on the server.”

Opinion

Timeline event:

“Someone probably forgot to clean out old files on the server.”

Timeline event:

“The server reported an ‘out of disk space’ error.”

Fact

Timeline event:

“The server reported an ‘out of disk space’ error.”

Key Point: Facts are objective information – information that is measurable or observable by others.
